Post Mortem

In terms of next steps, the most immediate area for improvement is the delicate design of map difficulty and balancing. Currently the game functions a bit like a sandbox: if you place enough towers you can usually brute-force a win. We want to evolve this into a more puzzle-like experience where maps are crafted with specific intended solutions‚Äîlimiting the number of viable strategies so players have to think about placement and synergies instead of spamming units.

Another area we want to improve is mid-game tower upgrading. Allowing players to enhance towers after placement would add a new layer of strategy and progression, giving the game a sense of escalation rather than a static defense setup. Implementing this system would require designing upgrade paths, costs, and effects that interact meaningfully with existing mechanics, rather than simply increasing raw damage. Ideally, upgrades would present players with interesting trade-offs‚Äîwhether to invest in improving a few core towers or spread resources across many weaker ones‚Äîand this could create more tension and planning within each run.

Lastly, I think the appearance of towers and bloons could be improved to make the game more visually appealing and engaging. At the moment, the art is functional but fairly simple, and enhancing these assets would make the game feel more polished and attractive to new players. This could involve refining shapes, colors, or small animations that make objects look more lively and satisfying to interact with.
